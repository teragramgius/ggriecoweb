§ II — How I work

A short note on method.

Much of what the GAF Unit produces — briefing notes, analytical summaries, survey-based reports, position papers — lives in the space between institutional language, empirical evidence and practical recommendations. It requires someone who can sit with complexity long enough to structure it, and then write it plainly.

My own approach rests on three simple commitments: take the source material seriously (read the regulation, the data, the partner's input before paraphrasing); design for the reader who will actually use the text (a ministry official, a rector, a project officer — not a generic audience); and keep the scaffolding visible (structure, citations, method notes), so that the work can be extended, reviewed, or challenged by others.

It is the approach I used for KALEIDOS D4.1, and the one I would bring to EUA's funding and governance work.
